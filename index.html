⚠️ The recurring "Incorrect tracking information" defect — what's happening
Each FC shows 30–37 box-level defects, all the same: Incorrect tracking information (small-parcel shipments). Critically: 0 shipment-level and 0 product-level defects, $0.00 fees. Amazon received everything anyway. A process flag, not a lost-inventory or charge event.

Why it recurs on China deliveries: with UPS DDP, the per-box tracking Amazon expects doesn't reconcile at scan — usually because (1) boxes travel under a consolidated master air waybill, so per-box tracking doesn't resolve to each FBA box ID; (2) the DDP broker re-labels parcels domestically after customs, breaking the original tracking chain; or (3) one tracking number is entered against many boxes in the plan.

Fix going forward: require the forwarder to supply a unique, scannable tracking number per carton, entered per-box before delivery — or switch to Amazon Partnered Carrier (UPS), where tracking auto-populates and this defect disappears. For now: Acknowledge the box defects (no penalty) and watch IPI — repeated defects can eventually trigger fees/delays even though they haven't yet.
